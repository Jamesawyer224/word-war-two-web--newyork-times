The Story of World War II

Nazi German Army and Their Technology

The Nazi German army, also known as the Wehrmacht, was one of the most technologically advanced military forces during World War II. The Wehrmacht's use of innovative strategies and cutting-edge technology played a significant role in their early successes during the war. The Blitzkrieg tactic, which combined rapid movement of infantry, tanks, and air support, allowed them to conquer large swathes of Europe in a short period. The Wehrmacht was equipped with some of the most advanced tanks, including the Panzer IV and the Tiger tank, which were known for their firepower and armor. Additionally, the Luftwaffe, the aerial warfare branch of the Wehrmacht, was equipped with state-of-the-art aircraft such as the Messerschmitt Bf 109 and the Focke-Wulf Fw 190, dominating the skies in the early years of the war.

1939: The Beginning

World War II begins with Germany's invasion of Poland on September 1st. The UK and France declare war on Germany.

1941: Pearl Harbor

The United States enters the war after Japan's attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7th.

1944: D-Day

D-Day, the Allied invasion of Normandy, occurs on June 6th, marking a turning point in the war.

1945: The End

Germany surrenders on May 7th, and Japan surrenders on September 2nd, officially ending the war.
